The Hidden Language of Nazi Propaganda

This project examines how the Nazi regime used posters and mass media to reshape public thinking, normalize discrimination, and promote an ideology of racial hierarchy. Propaganda was not passive it was a deliberate system of emotional and visual manipulation designed to make extremist policies appear rational, necessary, and patriotic.

The Reich Ministry of Public Enlightenment and Propaganda

Under Joseph Goebbels, this ministry unified control over newspapers, radio, art, film, literature, and public messaging. By deciding what Germans could see, hear, and read, the ministry created a world where Nazi ideology seemed natural and widely supported.
